§ 01 — The record

One record. Everything the building remembers.

Documents, systems, money and people — captured once, kept current, and handed cleanly to the next owner, trade or inspector who needs it.
01

Documents & deeds

Title, leases, permits, insurance, warranties — the legal spine of the property, filed and findable.

02

Systems & equipment

Every major system and appliance with model, serial, install date and warranty status.

03

Maintenance & work log

What broke, who fixed it, what it cost — a service history that compounds over years.

04

Finances & basis

Costs, capital improvements and tax basis tracked as they happen, not reconstructed later.

05

Inspections & compliance

Permits, code, safety and regulatory records — current status, not a shoebox of PDFs.

06

Contacts & trades

The vetted people who know the building — with ratings, last-job notes and what they charged.

07

Timeline

The whole story, in order — acquisition to today — exportable for partners, lenders and buyers.

08

Photos & plans

As-builts, floor plans, single-lines and site photography, attached to the place they describe.

§ 02 — How it works

Tag it. Scan it. Read the record.

A physical tag at the door, mailbox or service panel becomes the front door to everything the building knows about itself.
01

Tag the building

A durable, weatherproof tag carries a unique code and QR — placed at the entrance, panel or gate. One per property.

02

Scan at the door

Anyone with permission scans it on a phone — no app, no account hunt — and lands on the building's live record.

03

Read the right view

The record shows what each person needs: full history for the owner, the work order and shut-offs for a trade, the public file for an inspector.
